Vehicle Build Info:

Wire Harnesses: Bi-xenon: 'MotoControl 9003/H4/HB2

Owner Comments:

Could not be happier with this set-up, it absolutely blows any other lighting set-up out of the water! I was preparing my car for the 2012 Alcan 5000 Winter Rally, and we were faced with a number of challenges fitting the car with a bright enough lighting set-up without compromising vehicle cooling characteristics. This may seem like an odd thing to go hand-in-hand, however, after doing some testing ourselves and discussing with a few folks who have arctic BMW experience, we found out that the E36 cars do not have any problem whatsoever keeping at temperature in the harsh arctic environments we would be facing. While this is great from a performance point of view, the challenge we had was that by fitting traditional auxiliary lights on the car in front of the grille like on most other rally cars, we would cause the car to overheat. Roof mounted solutions were banned by regulations, and a hood pod was out of the question for aerodynamic reasons. By being able to combine the low and high beams into one of the two areas of the OEM housing, I was able to free up the old high beam spot to build my own, integrated, auxiliary lamps. Since the majority of the rally would be in pitch black, blowing snow, or extreme low-visibility situations, I needed to get the widest and longest throwing projector available, with the brightest set-up. The 4300K with 55W ballasts is super bright in both low and high be am configurations, and the 3000K bulbs in the auxiliary's cut through fog and snow like no other. A nice thing about the super sharp cut-off and even beam pattern in the FX-R's are that they don't glare back at you as much as regular reflector bowl halogens do in decreasing weather conditions. Reliability was extremely important as well, as nobody wants to be fixing broken headlights (or anything for that matter) at -40 below. Never encountered any sort of problems at all. Would definitely fit these again, they don't even compare to most OEM HID set-ups or aftermarket options. Our TRS retrofit was brighter and more reliable than anybody else's set-up on the Alcan 5000, even when compared to some of the very expensive rally-spec'd lighting pods some people had!

TRS Build Notes:

The mission: cross the finish line in the Alcan 5000 winter rally race in record time and in one piece. Not an easy feat! So when our customer Daniel began prepping his BMW M3 for the journey, he knew a set of high performance lights were a must. Having retrofitted his street car with parts from TRS, he was already familiar with the power of a proper projector system. We've suggested the FX-R bi-xenon projectors to many E36 BMW owners over the years, and knew they;'d be just the ticket for the job. Spec'd with the Morimoto 5Five 50w Ballasts and 5Five 4300K D2S bulbs, this setup produced over 8000 lumens of reliable and controlled light output.
